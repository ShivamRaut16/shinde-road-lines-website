About Us

Shinde Road Lines has been a leader in providing safe and on-time logistics and transportation solutions since 1987. With over 150 vehicles in our fleet and a legacy of trusted service, we are committed to delivering goods efficiently and reliably across India.

Our History

Shinde Road Lines was founded in 1987 by Mr. Kakasaheb Kondiba Shinde, who moved to Pune from Pingori, a small town in Purandhar Taluka, Pune district, Maharashtra, to begin his career in the transport industry. He started with a single truck, working with the B G Shirke Group to provide transport services between Pune and Mumbai. Over time, the company grew into a nationally renowned logistics and transport company, now owning a fleet of 150+ vehicles and managing over 100 attached vehicles. From its humble beginnings, Shinde Road Lines has become a key player in the Indian transport industry.

Our Leadership

Mr. Kakasaheb Shinde was joined by his wife, Mrs. Shaila Shinde, who played an integral role in managing the day-to-day operations, including banking, RTO work, and communications with drivers and business staff. In 2002, Mr. Manojkumar Shinde, son of Mr. Kakasaheb Shinde, joined the company, bringing fresh strategies and innovative marketing approaches. With over 19 years of experience in the transport industry, Mr. Manojkumar Shinde is responsible for overseeing operations. In 2018, Mr. Ajaykumar Shinde, with a degree in Mechanical Engineering, joined as Director. He oversees accounts, purchases, and maintenance operations, ensuring smooth day-to-day business activities. Mr. Ajaykumar Shinde holds certifications as an ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and OHSAS 18001 auditor and has expertise in policy implementation and 5S, Kaizen, and SMED tools. The company also benefits from the support of Mrs. Hemlata Ajaykumar Shinde (ME Computer), a professor in Engineering, who developed new software for the business, and Dr. Ashwini Manojkumar Shinde (MD), who manages the family hospital and contributes to employee wellness and medical policies.
